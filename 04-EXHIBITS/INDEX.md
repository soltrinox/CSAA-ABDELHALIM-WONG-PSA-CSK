## Key Evidentiary Issues

### 911 Call Recording

The 911 call recording contains Defendant Abdelhalim's party admission:
*"I barely hit him with anything."*

- Plaintiff's former counsel obtained this recording through a routine public records request
  within three months of the collision.
- CSAA issued its denial letter 21 days post-collision, claiming a "concluded investigation,"
  without ever obtaining this recording.
- At trial, defense counsel made false representations to the Court about the provenance
  and prior production history of this recording.
- The Court relied on those representations to impose procedural barriers on Plaintiff's
  presentation of the recording.

### Body-Worn Camera Footage

Body camera footage from the responding officers contained eyewitness statements material
to the liability question. The defense's treatment of this footage followed the same pattern
as the 911 call: false claims of non-receipt or unknown provenance.

### CAD Dispatch Records

Computer-aided dispatch logs provided a contemporaneous official record of the incident.
These were among the materials CSAA had not obtained before issuing its denial letter.
