## Abstract

On February 4, 2021, a vehicle struck Franciscus Dylan Rosario in a San Francisco crosswalk. The driver called 911 and said five words: *"I barely hit him with anything."* Those five words would become the most consequential evidence in a four-year legal war. Twenty-one days later, CSAA Insurance Exchange mailed a denial letter claiming it had "concluded" its investigation. It had not obtained the 911 recording, body-worn camera footage, or dispatch records. That was the first lie.

Four years later, at the threshold of trial, defense counsel told the court the evidence was "news to me" and had "not been produced in discovery." The materials had been in the defense file for nearly two years. That was the second lie. When defense counsel casually admitted on Trial Day 7 that the body-worn camera footage was "provided by Dolan," the artifice collapsed. The transcript captured both the lie and the confession. This is the story of how one brain-injured plaintiff, proceeding alone against a multi-billion-dollar insurer and its retained law firms, built from the defense's own record an architecture of accountability that turns every defense posture into a concession.

## Case Summary

### The Underlying Trial (CGC-21-594102)

Plaintiff Franciscus Dylan Rosario was struck by a vehicle driven by Defendant Subhi Abdelhalim. The jury returned a 9-3 defense verdict on April 23, 2025, before Judge Garrett L. Wong, Department 504, San Francisco Superior Court.

Plaintiff alleges the verdict was obtained through a scheme by which the defense made false representations to the Court about receipt and possession of critical evidence (911 call, body camera footage, CAD logs), causing the Court to deny continuances and impose procedural barriers based on those false representations.

**Operational linchpin:** On April 16, 2025 (Trial Day 7), defense counsel Priya D. Navaratnasingham stated: *"Sure. They were provided by Dolan. He has had them for whatever, sure."* This admission is irreconcilable with her prior representations that the materials were "not produced during discovery," "eleventh hour," and of "unknown provenance."
